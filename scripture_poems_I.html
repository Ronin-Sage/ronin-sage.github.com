:: "The Nature of a Soul" ::

You wonder, sometimes, if the souls are worth saving

There are moments, when the mind is dispelled, and the flesh abandoned
When the soul is revealed, raw and without feathers
Its skins no longer shielded from the common gaze by its cuirass of disposition
And its woven mesh no longer masking the pores of its wearer

Yet...

Were the blade of this scrutiny reversed
Would your fortitude against it be any stronger?
Who are you to condemn their bearings as a weakness
When you yourself armor the bearings of your being

-- so wholly

Sometimes you wonder, if we were all so naked
Would you ever wish to see again
