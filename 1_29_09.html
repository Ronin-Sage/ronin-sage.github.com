::"Makin' the Sell" -- January 29, 2009::

Most web-related jobs, as I've discovered, favor those with prepared portfolios as "proof" of their skills. The notion of building a portfolio has come to me before, naturally, but with limited professional experience and even less market-exposure in web content creation/management, I find myself without much tangible proof of the skills I've indicated in my recent flock of résumés a number of relative coding skills, as I can't build your "typical" webmaster porfolio.

It's not as if I've designed live, commercial sites as a freelancer (though I am more than capable of doing so, mind you), so what I plan to do is make a subsection in this site with code/design showcasing my skills with a small collection of sites I'll build for show-casing purposes. I'll either designate particularly shoddy sites already live on the web for local BR businesses and build revamped versions of them, or make sites for a "fake" business(complete with 555-5555 phone numbers) for job-marketing purposes.
