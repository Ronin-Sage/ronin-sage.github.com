++February 2009++

::"FOSS: part of the solution to the economic "crisis?" -- February 8, 2009::

FOSS(free & open-source software) has long been the "underappreciated underground" contender in the arena of PC software, but the recent global economic challenges may turn this "Little Mac" into a true contender, both for individual consumers as well as businesses.

When calculating the cost of a new computer from a major manufacturer or new office technology, the OS is a noticeable chunk of the sum. Then there's the cost of putting necessary productivity software on your machine(s)...which got me thinking--imagine how much money businesses and such would save if they opted to, for instance, go with Linux Mint 6, OpenOffice v3.x, and other packages? The average consumer would probably save a couple of hundred dollars, while enterprise-level establishment could save possibly tens of thousands by implementing a FOSS initiative.

Of course, there are two obvious obstacles. Most people would be generally uncomfortable abandoning Windows in favor an open platform. In retrospect, this is mainly so because they're scared of losing support for the likes of Microsoft Office, but obviously replacing it with FOSS alternatives would elminate this worry. Granted, there's a still a good bit of work to be done in terms of providing professional support for a lot of FOSS packages, but if I were an IT manager for a struggling firm, I sure would look into migrating to the critically more cost-effective area of free & open-source.
